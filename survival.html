⚙️ My Mechanical Engineering Life

By Yusralien | Student, Builder, Dreamer

How I Survived My Mechanical Engineering Degree (The Brutal Truth)

If you’re reading this, you’re probably drowning in assignments, battling terrible group members, or wondering why your lecturer speaks in an alien language. I get it—I’ve been there.

Surviving a Mechanical Engineering degree isn’t just about being smart. It’s about strategy, resilience, and knowing when to take matters into your own hands. Here’s how I made it through (without losing my sanity).

1. Survival Skill is the Key

Let’s be real: Engineering is a warzone. You don’t just need intelligence—you need tactics.

The Group Assignment Nightmare

  • Assume your group will slack. Hope for the best, but prepare to do 90% of the work.
  • Take charge early. Set deadlines before the actual deadline.
  • If they don’t contribute, cut your losses and do it yourself.
  • Document everything. If freeloaders try to take credit, receipts save your GPA.

Time Management = Survival

  • You can’t cram engineering. Break tasks into daily chunks.
  • Learn to say no. Social FOMO is real, but failing exams is worse.

2. Lecturer Terrible? YouTube is Your Savior

Not all lecturers are bad—some are godsends. But if yours is incomprehensible:

If Your Lecturer is Good:

  • Attend every class. Soak up their wisdom like a sponge.
  • Ask questions. They’re your best resource.

If Your Lecturer is Useless:

  • Still go to class (but don’t stress). Use lecture time to work on practice problems instead of zoning out.
  • YouTube is your real lecturer. Try:
    • Jeff Hanson – Mechanics
    • The Efficient Engineer – Concepts
    • Michel van Biezen – Problem-solving
  • Textbooks > Slides. A good book can save hours of confusion.

3. Assignments Will Try to Kill You (Here’s How to Fight Back)

Assignments aren’t just busywork—they’re exam prep in disguise.

Why 100% Commitment Matters:

  • They’re usually 30–50% of your grade. Half-effort = guaranteed regret.
  • Exam questions often come from assignments. Skip them and you’re doomed.

How to Actually Handle Assignments:

  • ✔ Start early. (Procrastination = all-nighters = pain.)
  • ✔ Do every problem—even the “optional” ones.
  • ✔ Stuck? Ask for help. Try friends, TAs, or Reddit.

Final Advice: It’s a Marathon, Not a Sprint

You’ll have days where you question your life choices. That’s normal. But remember:

  • Consistency beats last-minute panic.
  • Self-study > relying on bad lecturers.
  • Your degree is hard, but so are you.

If I survived, so can you. Now go crush those assignments.
